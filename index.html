I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics at North Carolina State University. I received my PhD in 2019 from UCLA, where I was advised by Terence Tao. Previously I was a postdoc at Indiana University Bloomington and the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

I am interested in harmonic analysis and its applications to combinatorics, number theory, and PDE. My research is supported by NSF Award DMS-2409803.

In Fall 2024, I am teaching Math 501: Advanced Mathematics for Engineers and Scientists I.

I am an organizer of the Stochastics/Discrete Analysis Seminar at NC State, the Online Analysis Research Seminar, and also the AIM Research Community on the Fourier restriction conjecture and related problems.
